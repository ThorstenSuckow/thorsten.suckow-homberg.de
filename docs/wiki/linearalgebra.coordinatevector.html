Coordinate Vector (Linear Algebra)

Let B={b1⃗,b2⃗,…,bn⃗}B = \{\vec{b_1}, \vec{b_2}, \ldots, \vec{b_n}\}B={b1​​,b2​​,…,bn​​} be a basis for the vector space V=RnV = \mathbb{R}^nV=Rn.

Then, every vector x⃗∈V\vec{x} \in Vx∈V can be written uniquely as a linear combination:

x=c1b1⃗+c2b2⃗+…+cnbn⃗x = c_1 \vec{b_1} + c_2 \vec{b_2} + \ldots + c_n \vec{b_n}x=c1​b1​​+c2​b2​​+…+cn​bn​​

The vector

[x⃗]B=(c1c2⋮cn) [\vec{x}]_B = \begin{pmatrix}c_1 \\ c_2 \\ \vdots \\ c_n \end{pmatrix}[x]B​=​c1​c2​⋮cn​​​

is then called the coordinate vector[📖LLM21, p. 256] of x⃗\vec{x}x relative to B.
