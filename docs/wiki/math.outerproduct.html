Outer Product

Let A\boldsymbol{A}A and B\boldsymbol{B}B be n×1n \times 1n×1-matrices.

The matrix product of

M=ABT\boldsymbol{M}= \boldsymbol{A} \boldsymbol{B}^TM=ABT

is called the outer product ([📖LLM21, p. 133]) of A\boldsymbol{A}A and B\boldsymbol{B}B.

Each entry Mij\boldsymbol{M}_{ij}Mij​ corresponds to the product of the iiith entry of A\boldsymbol{A}A and the jjjth entry of B\boldsymbol{B}B:

Mij=AiBj\boldsymbol{M}_{ij} = A_i B_jMij​=Ai​Bj​

Example​

(a1a2)(b1b2)=(a1b1a1b2a2b1a2b2)\begin{pmatrix} a_1 \\ a_2 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}b_1 & b_2\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} a_1 b_1 & a_1 b_2 \\ a_2 b_1 & a_2 b_2\end{pmatrix}(a1​a2​​)(b1​​b2​​)=(a1​b1​a2​b1​​a1​b2​a2​b2​​)

References
