# Antisymmetric Matrix

An **Antisymmetric Matrix** or **Skew Symmetric Matrix** is a matrix where its cross-diagonal entries are negated, that is

$$
\boldsymbol{A}_{ij} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{ji}
$$

Since


$$
\boldsymbol{A}_{ii} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{ii}
$$

it follows that the diagonal elements must be $0$ (<Cite name="Eve80" pp="31ff." />).

## Examples

#### Skew symmetric $4 \times 4$ matrix
Let

$$
\boldsymbol{A} = \begin{pmatrix}
 0 &  a & b & -c \\
-a &  0 & e & -h \\
-b & -e & 0 & -i \\
 c &  h & i & 0
\end{pmatrix}
$$

be a matrix. Then $\boldsymbol{A}$ is **skew symmetric**, since

- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{01} = a,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{10} = -a \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{10} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{01} $
- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{02} = b,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{20} = -b \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{20} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{02} $
- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{03} = -c,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{30} = c \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{30} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{03} $
- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{12} = e,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{21} = -e \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{21} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{12} $
- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{13} = -h,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{31} = h \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{31} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{13} $
- $ \boldsymbol{A}_{23} = -i,\ \boldsymbol{A}_{32} = i \Rightarrow \boldsymbol{A}_{32} = -\boldsymbol{A}_{23} $
- Diagonal: $ \boldsymbol{A}_{ii} = 0 $



#### Cross-product as skew-symmetric matrix
The **cross-product** of two vectors $\vec{a}, \vec{b} \in \mathbb{R}^3$ can be written as the Matrix-Vector-product of a skew-symmetric matrix and a vector:

Let

$$
\vec{a} = \begin{pmatrix}a_x\\a_y\\a_z\end{pmatrix},
\vec{b} = \begin{pmatrix}b_x\\b_y\\b_z\end{pmatrix}
$$

Then


$$
\begin{pmatrix}a_x\\a_y\\a_z\end{pmatrix} \times \begin{pmatrix}b_x\\b_y\\b_z\end{pmatrix} =
 \begin{pmatrix}a_y b_z - a_z b_y\\a_z b_x - a_x b_z\\a_x b_y - a_y b_x\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}
0 &- &a_z b_y &+ &a_y b_z  \\
a_z b_x &+   &0    &-&a_x b_z \\
-a_y b_x &+  &a_x b_y &+ &0
\end{pmatrix}  = \begin{pmatrix}
0 & -a_z & a_y  \\
a_z &   0  &  -a_x \\
-a_y &  a_x & 0
\end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix}
b_x \\
b_y \\
b_z
\end{pmatrix}
$$
