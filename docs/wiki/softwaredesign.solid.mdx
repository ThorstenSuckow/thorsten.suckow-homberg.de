# SOLID (Software Design)

**SOLID** is an acronym for five principles in Software Development and Software Design. They were introduced by
[Robert C. Martin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_C._Martin). His book **Agile Software Development - Principles, Patterns and Practices** <BibRef name="ASD" /> further elaborates on the principles.

The **SOLID**-principles are

 - <GlosRef name="SRP: Single Responsibility Principle" file="sd.singleresponsibilityprinciple" /> <BibRef name="ASD" pp="95"/>
 - **OCP: Open-Closed Principle**  <BibRef name="ASD" pp="99"/>
 - <GlosRef name="LSP: Liskov Substitution Principle" file="sd.liskovsubstitutionprinciple" />  <BibRef name="ASD" pp="111"/>
 - **ISP: Interface-Segregation Principle**  <BibRef name="ASD" pp="135"/>
 - <GlosRef name="DIP: Dependency-Inversion Principle" file="sd.dependencyinversionprinciple" />  <BibRef name="ASD" pp="127"/>
